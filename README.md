## English

### What this is

This repository contains a multilingual public health and agricultural knowledge document designed for free distribution in rural Senegalese communities and among the governmental, NGO, and research institutions that serve them. It covers four biological systems — moringa, duckweed, azolla, and spirulina — and their practical applications for nutrition, water security, soil regeneration, and fertilizer substitution in Sahel-context agriculture.

The document is available in French, English, Wolof, Pulaar, and Arabic.

### How it was made

This document was produced by Jesse Joel Rogers, a software developer and independent researcher based in South Florida, in collaboration with Mike Ba, a schoolteacher in Dagana, Senegal. It was researched, written, translated, and coded primarily using **Claude Sonnet 4.6**, an AI language model developed by Anthropic, rather than by a traditional research, development, and operations team.

This matters and you should understand what it means.

### Limitations — please read before using

**On information accuracy:** The scientific and agronomic claims in this document are drawn from peer-reviewed literature and documented field programs, and citations are provided throughout. However, this material was synthesized by an AI system, not verified by a credentialed agronomist, nutritionist, or public health professional. Specific figures — yield estimates, protein percentages, bacterial reduction rates — should be independently verified before being used as the basis for clinical or policy decisions. The authors have made every effort to ensure accuracy, but errors of interpretation, outdated data, and gaps in regional specificity are possible.

**On translation accuracy:** The Wolof, Pulaar, and Arabic translations were produced by Claude Sonnet 4.6. While significant effort was made to use documented vocabulary from verified linguistic sources, and specific terms were flagged for native speaker review in the source files, these translations have not been reviewed by certified professional translators or validated by community members in target regions. Wolof and Pulaar in particular involve complex grammatical structures, noun class systems, and regional dialect variation that an AI system cannot fully replicate. **Treat the Wolof and Pulaar translations as working drafts requiring community review before broad distribution in any context where mistranslation could cause harm.**

The French and English versions are the authoritative versions of this document.

**On medical and nutritional advice:** Nothing in this document constitutes medical advice. The nutritional information provided is for general educational purposes. Anyone using this material to inform clinical nutrition interventions, therapeutic feeding programs, or water treatment at scale should seek validation from qualified professionals.

## Français

### Ce que c'est

Il couvre quatre systèmes biologiques — le moringa, la lentille d'eau, l'azolla et la spiruline — et leurs applications pratiques pour la nutrition, la sécurité hydrique, la régénération des sols et la substitution des engrais dans le contexte agricole du Sahel.

Le document est disponible en français, en anglais, en wolof, en pulaar et en arabe.

Ce document a été produit par Jesse Joel Rogers, développeur logiciel et chercheur indépendant basé en Floride du Sud (États-Unis), en collaboration avec Mike Ba, enseignant à Dagana, au Sénégal.
